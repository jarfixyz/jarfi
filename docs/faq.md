# FAQ

---

**Is my money safe? Where is it stored?**

Your funds are held in a non-custodial smart contract on Solana — only you control them. Jarfi never has access to your money. The smart contract is open source.

---

**Do contributors need a crypto wallet?**

No. Contributors open a link and pay by Apple Pay, Google Pay, card, or bank transfer. No wallet, no sign-up, no crypto knowledge needed. The conversion happens automatically in the background.

---

**What is staking / yield?**

Staking means your funds are put to work in a DeFi lending protocol (Kamino) that earns interest. Jarfi does this automatically — you don't need to know anything about DeFi. Current estimated yield: 4–8% APY.

---

**How do I withdraw my money?**

When the jar unlocks (on the date you set, or when the goal is reached), click "Break jar & withdraw" in the jar detail. This runs two transactions: unlock + withdraw. USDC arrives in your wallet within seconds.

---

**Which countries and currencies are supported?**

Jarfi works globally. Contributors can pay in USD, EUR, GBP, UAH, and 40+ other currencies. The owner receives funds onchain in USDC. Available in 160+ countries via Transak.

---

**What if I lose access to my wallet?**

Because Jarfi is non-custodial, losing your wallet seed phrase means losing access to funds — just like any crypto wallet. Use a hardware wallet or a reputable wallet with cloud backup.

---

**Can I have multiple jars?**

Yes. Create as many jars as you need — one per goal, one per child, one for group savings, etc.

---

**What is the minimum contribution?**

The minimum is $15 per card payment (Transak minimum). There is no maximum.

---

**Is Jarfi free to use?**

Creating a jar costs a small Solana rent fee (~0.002 SOL ≈ $0.30). Transak charges a small processing fee on card payments. Jarfi itself does not charge fees.

---

**When is mainnet?**

Jarfi is currently on Solana devnet. Mainnet launch is planned after smart contract audit and security review.

---

**Can I cancel or break a locked jar early?**

For Goal and Date jars, the contract enforces the lock. You can only withdraw when the conditions are met. For Shared jars, you can withdraw anytime. Emergency break (bypassing lock) is not available for security reasons.

---

**How is yield distributed?**

Yield accrues continuously in Kamino. When you break the jar, you receive your principal plus all accumulated yield in a single USDC transfer to your wallet.
